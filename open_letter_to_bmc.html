26th February 2024

We write to you on behalf of the British climbing community, 75,000+ of whom have a membership with the British Mountaineering Council. And we write to you with a clear request: sanction the Israeli Association for Sport Climbing and Mountaineering (ILCA), and appeal to the IFSC and UIAA to do the same.

We remind you at this point of the BMC’s 2022 letter to the IFSC and UIAA, in which the BMC departs from its ordinarily apolitical stance, stating a total sanctioning of Russia and Belarus in climbing competitions. We implore the BMC to make this departure once again.

In reference to Russia, the letter states; “We do not believe that we can sit by and watch this atrocity unfold”. Indeed, we could not. And, again, we cannot sit by and watch Israel’s equally calculated atrocity unfold. To suggest otherwise - to render the above statement applicable to one population’s suffering while ignoring another - would be tantamount to a chilling hierarchising of the value of human lives.

For more than four months, Israel has waged a genocidal war against Palestinians in Gaza. To date, Euro-Med Human Rights Monitor reports that 38,066 Palestinians have been murdered, 22,970 of those women and children. Countless bodies lie under the rubble.

The indiscriminate bombing campaign and multiple ground offensives aside, Israel makes no secret of its intention to weaponise the requirements for human survival. It is ensuring that starvation, spread of disease, and lack of medical supplies accelerates the erasure of the Gazan population.

Israel’s blatant disregard for human decency and empathy is utterly condemnable. We list here some of the most heinous examples of Israel’s violation of international law and international humanitarian law since 7/10.*

  • UNICEF has named Israel’s attack on Gaza a “war on children” with an average of 160 children being killed a day.* The deliberate targeting of children is both a war crime and a crime against humanity under the Rome Statute of the ICC.* These deaths are not only the result of airstrikes; a number of videos circulating show the devastating impact of Israel having cut off electricity and fuel as well as forcing staff to evacuate. Infants have been left to die alone in multiple hospitals including al-Shifa and al-Nasr.
  • Blocking humanitarian relief and weaponising starvation is considered a war crime under the Rome Statute of the ICC, the Geneva Convention, and international humanitarian law.* At the time of writing, UNRWA reports that the last time food was delivered to northern Gaza was 23rd January. And there are currently multiple satellite images trending on social media showing more than 2,000 aid trucks stuck at the southern Rafah crossing.
  • The use of white phosphorous in densely populated areas violates the prohibition under international law of putting civilians unnecessarily at risk.* Yet, Israel regularly and indiscriminately exercises this technique of destruction, as reported by Amnesty International as far back as October, with more recent footage delivered by civilians on the ground in Gaza City.
  • Israel’s bombing of schools, universities and hospitals - protected sites under international humanitarian law - has been unprecedented in scale, with Israel offering a weak justification, accusing Hamas of keeping “military assets” under the buildings.* More than 200 schools have been damaged and, as of the 18th February, 23 hospitals in Gaza are not functioning at all, with 12 partially functioning.
  • Israel’s intentions have been clear from the beginning, proving intent towards the “serious risk of genocide” which 880 scholars warned the international community of only seven days into the attack on Gaza.* To date, these have been some of the most chilling uses of genocidal language:
“There are no innocents in Gaza”
~ Avigdor Lieberman, leader of Yisrael Beiteinu party
“The children of Gaza have brought this upon themselves…”
~ Merav Ben-Ari, Knesset member
“...We are fighting against human animals, and we are acting accordingly”
~ Yoav Gallant, Israeli Defence Minister
“We will turn Khan Yunis into a soccer field…”
~ Ayelet Shaked, Former Israeli Minister of Justice
“I am personally proud of the ruins of Gaza…”
~ May Golan, Israeli Minister

We are compelled to make it clear that, even without the points listed above, Israel has been in breach of international law for decades.

Amnesty International writes that “The legacy of Israel’s [56]-year occupation of the Palestinian territories has been systematic human rights violations on a mass scale”.* For Gaza, the withdrawal of troops and settlers in 2005 did not result in any level of liberation. Instead, the siege intensified, with Israel imposing sea and air blockades, along with the construction of its 60km “Iron Wall”, stretching the entire boundary between Israel and the Gaza strip. In the West Bank, the occupation continues to the present day with illegal settlements mushrooming alongside increased settler violence, a clear apartheid regime in place, and a horrifying number of children abducted by the Israeli army every year.

Our letter comes at a critical juncture; Israel’s plans for an imminent ground offensive in Rafah is the most screamingly acute example of a State hell-bent on the elimination of one population in favour of its own. Like bullies in a playground, Israel has cornered over a million Palestinians in a place it once - in an act of malicious gaslighting - designated a “safe zone”. The potential consequences of this offensive are too significant to comprehend.

With Russia, and indeed apartheid South Africa, as precedents, it is clear that the only appropriate response of any sporting community to Israel’s deplorable actions is to suspend the State from all global and European sporting events. To quote the BMC’s letter in reference to Russia once more; “the international community [can] not stand idly by in the face of such aggression”.

We remind the BMC of its commitment to equality, as described in its equity statement. This commitment necessarily compels action against egregious racist and fascist behaviour, such as that of Israel’s. And such a commitment must also extend to the well-being and safety of climbers worldwide. The Palestinian climbing community is undergoing a period of accelerated change and restriction in the West Bank in response to 7/10. It is further inhibiting their ability to climb and live freely. For us to truly uphold the values of fair treatment and equal opportunities, we must not leave that community behind.

Make no mistake, the present moment is unlike any we have witnessed before. This is the world’s first livestreamed genocide. It is replete with traumatic coverage of civilian slaughter, complemented by what is now described as snuff content, gleefully uploaded by Israeli soldiers to social media channels. Inaction in the face of such unflinching evidence of injustice can only be interpreted as complicity.

Lastly, the IFSC recently admitted Palestine as a member. This indicates a welcome shift in paradigm that we urge the BMC to use as an opportunity to outline a firm stance. We request that the BMC refuse to host Israeli delegates in British competitions, abstain from competing anywhere where athletes can take part under the name of Israel, and cut ties with any sponsorship linked to Israel. Let the IFSC know that, in no uncertain terms, the British climbing community stands against genocide and ethnic cleansing. The green light that Israel has enjoyed thus far must be turned red.

Yours,
Climbers for Palestine

*Please refer to Diem25’s appeal to suspend Israel from International Sports for more sources
